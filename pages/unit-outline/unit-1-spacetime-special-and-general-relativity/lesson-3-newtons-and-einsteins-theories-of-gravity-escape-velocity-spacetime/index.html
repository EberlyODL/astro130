What is Gravity according to Newton?

In simple terms, gravity is an attractive force that acts between two objects.

Any object that has mass pulls on all other objects in the universe that have mass. We call this force gravity.

The force of gravity depends on the masses of the two bodies, and their separation squared;

  • the larger the mass, the greater the attraction;
  • the larger the separation, the smaller the attraction.
