Einstein showed that space and time are not independent, but are inextricably linked in a four-dimensional entity called spacetime.  Spacetime is the four-dimensional combination of space and time that forms the “fabric” of our universe.

The universe has three spatial dimensions (length, width, height) and one time dimension.  Observers in relative motion do not share the same definitions of length, width, height and time (x, y, z, and t), taken individually:

Space is different for different observers.

Time is different for different observers.

Spacetime is the same for everyone.

For example, a book has a definite three-dimensional shape. But the book looks different in two-dimensional pictures of the book taken from different perspectives.

Similarly, space and time look different from different perspectives in spacetime.

It is impossible for us to picture four dimensions, as we are used to 3D.  However, we can use an example with two dimensions, like a rubber sheet, where by curving the rubber sheet, we can picture an extra dimension.
