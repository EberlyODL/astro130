Why Do We Need Relativity?

For standard situations like throwing a ball up in the air and describing how and when it will fall down, Newton’s Laws work just fine. However, when we talk about the behavior of an electron in an atom, or the gravitational field of a black hole or even our GPS systems, Newton’s laws are not accurate enough and we need Special & General Relativity. Newton’s Laws are actually an approximation and the inaccuracies we introduce by using them are so small that they don’t matter for everyday situations.

In Astronomical situations where we encounter huge masses, densities, or velocities near the speed of light we need to use Special & General Relativity to explain such behavior.

Question 2

How fast is the train moving relative to the stationary observer?

  1. Reveal the answer

    The train is moving at 40 mph with respect to the stationary observer.

Question 3

How fast is the ‘walker’ moving relative to the train?

  1. Reveal the answer

    The walker is moving 10 mph with respect to the train.

Question 4

How fast is the ‘walker’ moving relative to the stationary observer?

  1. Reveal the answer

    Since the train and the walker are moving in opposite directions, a stationary observer must subtract their velocities. The observer would see the walker moving at 40 mph – 10 mph = 30 mph.
